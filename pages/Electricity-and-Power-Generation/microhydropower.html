Microhydropower

Basic information:Microhydropower is a renewable energy technology that generates electricity from flowing water, typically utilizing small-scale hydroelectric systems. It harnesses the kinetic energy of water in streams or rivers to drive a turbine connected to a generator, producing electricity. Microhydropower systems are often used to power remote homes, farms, or small communities, offering a reliable and environmentally friendly source of energy.

Cost:Small-scale microhydropower systems for home usage typically range in cost from around $5,000 to $50,000, depending on factors such as system size, site conditions, and installation complexity.
